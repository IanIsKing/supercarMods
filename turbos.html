Why the Garrett G35 Turbos?

The Lamborghini V10 engine is a masterpiece of engineering and performance, but there is always room for improvement. Turbocharging has long been a popular choice for car enthusiasts who crave even more power and speed from their vehicles. Among the numerous turbocharger options, one combination that stands out is the pairing of two Garrett G35 turbos with the Lamborghini V10 engine. This article will delve into the reasons why this combination is a match made in heaven and provide some insights into the performance gains you can expect.
The Garrett G35 turbochargers are designed to provide outstanding performance and reliability for engines producing up to 900 horsepower per turbo. These turbos feature a dual ball-bearing design, which ensures exceptional spooling, improved efficiency, and reduced lag compared to conventional journal bearing turbos. The G35 series also benefits from advanced aerodynamics and a high-flow compressor, further contributing to the turbo's ability to generate impressive power gains.
The Lamborghini V10 engine, with its unique 90-degree cylinder configuration and compact design, is an ideal candidate for twin Garrett G35 turbos. The turbos' characteristics complement the V10's high-revving nature and robust heat management, resulting in a harmonious combination that significantly enhances the engine's power output.


Performance Expectations

With twin Garrett G35 turbochargers installed on a Lamborghini V10 engine, the performance gains can be substantial. While exact figures may vary depending on factors such as supporting modifications and tuning, you can generally expect the following improvements:

  1. Horsepower: The Lamborghini V10 engine can produce anywhere from 500 to 640 horsepower (373 to 477 kW), depending on the specific model. With twin G35 turbos and proper tuning, you can expect an increase of 40% to 60% in horsepower, potentially reaching over 1000 horsepower (746 kW) in some cases.
  2. Torque: The V10 engine delivers impressive torque figures ranging from 509 to 601 Nm. With the addition of twin Garrett G35 turbos, torque can be increased by 30% to 50%, resulting in a significantly more powerful low- and mid-range response.
  3. Acceleration: The increased horsepower and torque from the turbochargers will lead to improved acceleration. While a stock Lamborghini V10 might sprint from 0 to 100 km/h in 2.8 to 3.5 seconds, you can expect to shave off 0.5 to 1 second with the twin G35 setup, pushing the vehicle into the elite group of hypercars.
  4. Top Speed: While the top speed of a stock Lamborghini V10 can reach between 306 and 338 km/h, adding twin Garrett G35 turbos can push the top speed well beyond 354 km/h, placing it in the realm of the world's fastest production vehicles.
